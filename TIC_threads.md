## ENTITY VERDICT: PARTIALLY HOLDS — real phenomena, consistently inflated; and the subordination claim runs backwards

The three TIC components are real: extreme index concentration, genuine algorithmic-influence capability, and the
named firms/infrastructure exist. But each is **stretched**: the concentration number is exaggerated (~35% spoken as
"almost 50%"), the algorithmic-influence capability is over-attributed to a single coordinating "complex," and the
"subordinate to finance" claim is **largely backwards** — the cash-rich majority self-fund. Notably, TIC is the one
place Dixon's master hierarchy (FIC-above-all) partially **inverts**: these firms are closer to financial peers than
subordinates. **Pattern: real substrate, inflated magnitude, mis-assigned agency.**

## TIC-1 — "Almost 50% of the S&P 500 is the Magnificent Seven"
- **N1** the Magnificent Seven (Apple, Microsoft, Alphabet, Amazon, Nvidia, Meta, Tesla) are an extreme,
  record-high share of the S&P 500 → **Verified** (the concentration story is real; ~12% a decade ago).
- **N2** the share is "almost 50%" → **Falsified as stated.** Actual ≈ **34–35%** (peak ~34.5% Aug 2025, ~34.3%
  Dec 2025; intraday quotes occasionally near 36%, never near 50%). Overstated by ~15 points.
- **EDGE** ("a few tech names dominate → TIC is a coherent power bloc") → **Weak.** Concentration is real;
  "coordinated complex" is the interpretive add-on.
- **Verdict: PARTIALLY HOLDS.** Striking real fact inflated by ~40%. Same signature as his "Mag-7 ≈ 50%" stat
  flagged in the original design trace — a recurring inflation tic worth noting for a confidence score.

## TIC-2 — "Engineer perception via 'neo media' and perform operations by controlling algorithms"
- **N1** recommendation algorithms shape what users see / amplify content and echo chambers → **Verified**
  (peer-reviewed work; Congressional Research Service brief).
- **N2** platforms are used for coordinated influence operations → **Verified** (Meta adversarial-threat reports:
  takedowns of Russia/China/Iran state-linked networks; the "Doppelganger" network).
- **N3** "*the complex* engineers perception" (unified, top-down agency) → **Unverifiable / interpretive.** The
  documented operators are mostly hostile *state* actors and the platforms policing them — not a single coordinated
  technological-industrial complex directing perception.
- **EDGE** ("capability exists → a coordinating complex wields it") → **Weak/Unwarranted.** The mechanism is real;
  the coordinated-agency leap is asserted, not shown.
- **Verdict: PARTIALLY HOLDS.** Capability `Verified`; the conspiratorial agency is the unverifiable overreach.

## TIC-3 — "These tech companies are subordinate to finance because they need access to capital"
- **N1** Apple/Microsoft/Alphabet/Meta depend on external capital markets → **Source Says Otherwise.** They are
  among the most cash-generative firms on earth (Alphabet >$100B cash; Apple/MSFT/Meta >$50B each) and historically
  self-fund. The dependency framing inverts the actual power balance.
- **N2** *recent* AI-capex debt issuance (Oracle $18B Sep-25 + $25B Feb-26; Meta record $30B Oct-25; Amazon $15B
  Nov-25; Alphabet $20B Feb-26; hyperscalers ~$108B debt in 2025) → **Verified**, and a real, timely kernel — finance
  has *more* leverage over Big Tech than a year ago.
- **EDGE** ("they tap debt markets → they are subordinate to finance") → **Unwarranted** for the cash-rich majority.
  Elective leverage from strength ≠ structural subordination; they could halt borrowing and still self-fund.
- **Verdict: FAILS as stated, thin recent kernel.** This is the claim that runs backwards — and it's load-bearing
  for Dixon's "FIC sits above TIC" hierarchy (cross-ref FIC-6).

## TIC-4 — Components: data centers, algorithms, the Magnificent Seven
