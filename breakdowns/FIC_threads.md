## FIC-1 — "Money is created as credit by private commercial banks"
**Claim:** *"Money is created by a private commercial bank… a loan has to exist for a bank to create credit… it's just a digital entry in a bank's computer."*
- **N1** banks create money when they lend (loan creates a matching deposit) → **Verified.** Bank of England,
  *Money creation in the modern economy* (2014): "whenever a bank makes a loan, it simultaneously creates a
  matching deposit… thereby creating new money." Explicitly rejects the loanable-funds / money-multiplier story.
- *Caveat node:* banks don't create *all* money (central bank issues base money/reserves; lending constrained by
  capital, regulation, demand) → minor, doesn't dent the claim.
- **Verdict: HOLDS (Verified).** This is now standard central-bank description, not a fringe view. Fairness check:
  the conspiracy-coded claim is actually correct.

## FIC-2 — "Central banking is a structural Ponzi — not enough money exists to pay the interest"
**Claim:** *"It needs to be repaid plus interest… there's not enough money to pay the interest… the system must grow at all times or it breaks."*
- **N1** broad money is created as debt and destroyed on repayment; system has a credit-expansion bias → **Partially Verified** (real kernel; cf. BIS/Minsky on debt cycles).
- **N2** "there is not enough money in existence to pay the interest" → **Source Says Otherwise / Falsified.** Known
  accounting fallacy: it treats the money *stock* as fixed, but interest is paid from circulating *income flows*
  (bank interest income is re-spent as wages/dividends/costs), and new lending replenishes the stock. Interest is
  a flow, not a one-time stock that must pre-exist.
- **N3** "Ponzi scheme" framing → **Falsified as a technical match.** A Ponzi has no productive assets and pays
  old investors from new principal; a credit-money economy is backed by real output/assets. Rhetorical analogy, not
  a structural identity.
- **EDGE** ("debt-money → therefore mathematically must collapse/grow forever") → **Unwarranted.** The growth-bias
  concern is legitimate; the "impossible to repay" mechanism it rests on is false, so the catastrophic inference
  isn't licensed.
- **Verdict: FAILS at the core, real kernel attached.** Debt-based + growth-biased = fair. "Not enough money to
  pay interest / Ponzi" = debunked. This is the FIC thread where Dixon's signature claim is weakest.

## FIC-3 — "Three central banks in America"
- **N1** First Bank (1791–1811), Second Bank (1816–1836), Federal Reserve (1913–) → **Verified.** The Fed itself
  is conventionally described as the third (Minneapolis Fed history; Fed History essays).
- *Caveat:* First/Second were quasi-public and lacked some modern central-bank powers — "central bank" applied
  loosely — but "three" is the standard, defensible count.
- **Verdict: HOLDS (Verified).**

## FIC-4 — "Engineered first in the Dutch Empire, then British, then three times in America, then global"
- **N1** Dutch financial pioneering (Bank of Amsterdam 1609; VOC exchange 1602) → **Verified** as influential, but
  the Wisselbank was a municipal exchange bank, not a true central bank.
- **N2** Bank of England (1694) as the modern central-bank template, copied across the Empire → **Verified.**
- **N3** US → three successive institutions; 20th-c. proliferation + BIS (1930)/IMF (1944) → **Verified.**
- **N4** framed as a single deliberate "engineered" succession → **Partially Verified / overstated.** It's an
  evolutionary diffusion, not a designed continuous project (and omits e.g. Sweden's Riksbank 1668).
- **EDGE** ("lineage → proof of intentional design") → **Weak.** Real diffusion; "engineered" smuggles in intent.
- **Verdict: PARTIALLY HOLDS.** Directionally right history, too tidy on intent.

## FIC-5 — BlackRock cluster
**Claim bundle:** *BlackRock is "the most important node in the West"; Aladdin controls ~$25T; it's used by "every
pension fund… insurance company… sovereign wealth fund… central bank and treasury"; "Larry Fink was the inventor"
of the MBS; "Blythe Masters was the inventor of the credit default swap"; the Aramco CEO got a BlackRock board seat.*
- **N1** Aladdin "controls $25 trillion in capital allocation" → **Partially Verified / Source Says Otherwise.**
  ~$21–25T is assets *monitored / risk-managed* on the platform by 200+ external clients — **not** controlled or
  allocated by BlackRock. BlackRock's own investable AUM ≈ $11.6T (end-2024) → ~$14T (end-2025). "Controls" is the
  inflation; the platform models risk, clients keep allocation control.
- **N2** "every central bank / SWF / endowment / treasury uses Aladdin" → **Partially Verified (heavy hyperbole).**
  Genuinely dominant among large managers/insurers; some official institutions use it (ECB/Fed engaged BlackRock in
  crisis programs; Bank of Israel cited). "Every… around the world" is categorically false.
- **N3** "Larry Fink invented the mortgage-backed security / it was an innovation of BlackRock" → **Falsified.** MBS
  predates Fink (Ginnie Mae pass-through, 1970). Fink *pioneered the CMO* at **First Boston** (~1983, Freddie Mac
  collateral) — before BlackRock existed (founded 1988). Kernel (CMO pioneer) → Misattributed, not "inventor of MBS."
- **N4** "Blythe Masters invented the credit default swap" → **Verified** (standard "led the JPMorgan team, mid-1990s"
  caveat; landmark 1994 Exxon/EBRD deal).
- **N5** "Aramco CEO got a BlackRock board seat" → **Verified.** Amin Nasser, independent director, effective July 2023.
- **Verdict: MIXED.** Two clean Verifieds (Masters, Nasser), one Falsified (Fink/MBS), and the marquee "$25T controls"
  is the classic monitor-vs-control inflation. BlackRock's real centrality is conceded; the *control* magnitude is overstated.

## FIC-6 — "Everything is subordinate to finance because they need capital" (the hierarchy)
- **N1** MIC firms are public and need capital markets → **Verified** (see MIC doc, thread #2).
- **N2** TIC firms are subordinate to finance because they need capital → **Source Says Otherwise.** The cash-rich
  giants (Apple/Microsoft/Alphabet/Meta) self-fund; the 2025–26 AI-debt binge gives a thin, recent kernel but is
  elective leverage from strength, not dependence (see TIC doc, TIC-3).
- **EDGE** ("all complexes need capital → FIC sits above all") → **Weak.** True for MIC, overstated for cash-rich TIC.
  The pyramid is real at the base, leaky at the top.
- **Verdict: PARTIALLY HOLDS.** The capital-dependence lever is real for some nodes, not universal.

## FIC-7 — "Government is just another bondholder / a piggy bank for bondholders; politicians are actors"

- **Verdict: framing, not fact.** Park the rhetoric; the bond-discipline kernel is sound.
